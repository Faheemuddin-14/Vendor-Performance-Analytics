This repository contains an end-to-end **Vendor Performance Analytics** project designed to evaluate vendor efficiency, brand profitability, and inventory performance in a retail/wholesale business context. The project demonstrates how data analytics can support **pricing strategy, vendor management, inventory optimization, and revenue growth**.

---

## üéØ Business Problem

Retail and wholesale companies often face challenges such as:

* Over-reliance on a small number of vendors
* High inventory holding costs due to slow-moving products
* Inconsistent profit margins across brands
* Inefficient pricing and purchasing strategies

This project aims to:

* Identify **top and low-performing vendors and brands**
* Analyze **purchase contribution and vendor dependency risk**
* Detect **slow-moving inventory and unsold capital**
* Compare **profitability models** between high- and low-performing vendors
* Support data-driven decisions for **pricing, promotions, and procurement**

## üìä Key Metrics Analyzed

* Total Sales

---

## üîç Key Insights

* **Top 10 vendors contribute ~66% of total purchases**, indicating supplier concentration risk
* **$2.71M in unsold inventory capital**, impacting cash flow and storage costs
* **Low-performing vendors maintain higher profit margins (~41.5%)** but lower sales volume
* **Bulk purchasing reduces unit costs by ~72%**, reinforcing volume-based procurement strategies
* **198 brands identified** with low sales but high margins, ideal for pricing or promotional optimization
